What is HELP?

HELP is a non-profit Therapeutic Riding Center for people with disabilities. We teach horsemanship and horseback riding skills to improve the lives and life skills of people with a wide range of challenges and disabilities. Our focus is on building a connection, increasing independence, improving social and cognitive skills while learning horsemanship and horseback riding in a safety-centered environment based on PATH International Standards.

HELP has been serving the Austin area since 1984 on our beautiful 20 acre facility just outside of Pflugerville. We not only use our arena, but are blessed with a 5 acre natural riding trail and a barn built by the Corps of Engineers in 1999.

HELP is a registered 501c3 non-profit organization. We serve individuals to promote their physical, emotional, and social growth in partnership with the horse.

Since 1984, HELP has provided a safe and caring environment for people with challenges to experience the joy of equine partnership.

Impact Statement

HELP reaches clients from near and far! Our central location makes us accessible for clients from all over Travis and Williamson counties. The Therapeutic Riding HELP provides is a unique way for our clients to improve their life skills including physical, emotional, and cognitive in partnership with their horse and special team of volunteers. We work on social interaction (greeting horse and volunteers as well as saying Thanks and Goodbye at the end of the session), we teach horsemanship to the level the client can accomplish (brushing, tacking, getting the horse from the pasture, and so much more!) which improves confidence. All sessions at HELP are under the safety standards and guidelines of PATH International, an international certifying organization with the focus on safety and improving life skills.
